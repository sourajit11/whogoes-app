## What Technology and SaaS Buyers Are Hunting for at LEAP This Year

LEAP isn't CES. The crowd isn't there to gawk at concept products and take selfies with robots. The buying intent at LEAP is real, backed by government mandates and sovereign wealth fund allocations that make Western enterprise budgets look modest.

Three themes dominate the buyer conversations at LEAP 2026:

**AI infrastructure and implementation.** Saudi Arabia isn't just talking about AI. HUMAIN (the national AI company) launched at LEAP 2025 with billions in committed compute capacity. Buyers at LEAP 2026 are looking for AI orchestration platforms, MLOps tooling, and vertical AI applications they can deploy within Saudi regulatory frameworks. If you sell anything adjacent to LLM deployment, inference optimization, or AI governance, this is your crowd.

Buyers need solutions that comply with local regulations while maintaining enterprise-grade security. This creates opportunity for any SaaS vendor that can demonstrate in-Kingdom data processing.

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF) operates dozens of portfolio companies: NEOM, ROSHN, Red Sea Global, Qiddiya. Each one has a massive technology procurement mandate. The people making those decisions attend LEAP, but they don't always list "PIF" on their LinkedIn. They're at the portfolio companies. Look for titles like "Chief Digital Officer" or "VP Technology" at organizations you haven't heard of, that report up to sovereign wealth.

International SIs (Accenture, Deloitte, EY) all have Saudi operations with specific Vision 2030 delivery contracts. Their local teams attend LEAP to evaluate vendor partnerships. The MENA startup ecosystem has matured. Their engineering and product leaders attend LEAP looking for tooling: observability, CI/CD, security, customer data platforms. They move fast and don't have legacy procurement bureaucracy.

## LEAP 2026 vs GITEX, Web Summit, and CES: Why the Riyadh Crowd Is Different

SDRs often ask: "I already work GITEX. Do I need LEAP too?" Short answer: yes. Different crowd, different buying behavior.

| Location | Riyadh | Dubai | Lisbon/Vancouver | Las Vegas |
| Primary audience | Enterprise + government buyers | Enterprise IT | Startups + media | Consumer electronics |

**GITEX skews Dubai and UAE.** If your market is the broader Gulf, GITEX matters. But Saudi Arabia is the largest economy in the region by far, and LEAP is where Saudi buyers go. The government representation alone makes the two events complementary, not interchangeable.

**Web Summit is media and startups.** Great for brand awareness and fundraising. Terrible for enterprise sales. The buyer profile barely overlaps with LEAP.

**CES is consumer-facing.** Unless you sell to hardware OEMs or consumer brands, CES won't give you MENA enterprise contacts.

The overlap between LEAP and GITEX is maybe 15-20% by attendee. Most people attend one or the other based on their primary market (Saudi vs. UAE). I've talked to SDR teams who cover both, but they run completely different playbooks for each. The messaging that works for a Dubai-based VP at GITEX falls flat with a Riyadh-based government buyer at LEAP.

## When Is LEAP 2026 and How to Time Your Outreach

LEAP 2026 runs **August 31 to September 3, 2026** at the Riyadh Exhibition and Convention Centre in Malham.

Your outreach timeline should account for Riyadh's calendar. August in Saudi Arabia is still summer, but business activity picks up after the Hajj period. Most Saudi enterprises have their H2 budgets approved by late August, making LEAP perfectly timed for new vendor evaluations.

Recommended outreach windows:
- **6 weeks before (mid-July)**: First touch. "I noticed you're attending LEAP" works now since early registrations and LinkedIn posts are appearing
- **3 weeks before (early August)**: Follow up with session-specific context. Reference specific tracks or speakers
- **1 week before**: Short, meeting-focused. "Can I grab 15 minutes at LEAP?" No long pitches
- **During the event**: Real-time, booth or session references
- **48 hours after**: Recap email referencing something specific from the event

Side note: Riyadh operates on Arabia Standard Time (AST, UTC+3). Schedule your emails to land between 9-11am AST for maximum open rates. That's 6-8am GMT or 2-4am Eastern. Worth setting up if you're running automated sequences.
