## What Were the Key Themes at HIMSS 2026?

This is where your outreach gets specific. Referencing actual sessions and themes from the conference turns a generic "I saw you were at HIMSS" into something that lands.

The big themes this year:

- **AI Governance in Clinical Settings**: This was everywhere. Multiple sessions covered responsible AI deployment in hospitals, including ambient clinical documentation (the tech that listens to doctor-patient conversations and auto-generates notes) and clinical decision support. If you sell anything AI-related to health systems, this is your hook.

- **Cybersecurity for Health Systems**: Ransomware attacks targeting healthcare hit record levels in 2025, and the CISO-focused sessions at HIMSS 2026 were packed. Security vendors had some of the busiest booths on the floor.

- **TEFCA and Interoperability**: The [Trusted Exchange Framework and Common Agreement](https://www.healthit.gov/topic/interoperability/trusted-exchange-framework-and-common-agreement-tefca) continues to reshape how health data flows between organizations. If you work in health data exchange, this is the conversation everyone's having.

- **Revenue Cycle Automation**: CFOs and operations leaders showed up in force to explore AI-driven coding, prior authorization automation, and denial management. The financial pressure on health systems is real, and they're looking for anything that moves the needle.

Use these themes in your outreach. "I saw you attended the AI governance sessions at HIMSS, we're working on exactly that problem" beats "I saw you were at HIMSS" every single time.

## How Do I Get the HIMSS 2026 Attendee List?

Five main routes, each with different tradeoffs.

### 1. Request It from the [Event Organizer](/compare/whogoes-vs-event-attendee-lists)

HIMSS shares attendee data with sponsors and exhibitors under specific agreements. If your company had a booth or a sponsorship, you may already have access to lead retrieval data from badge scans.

**Cost**: Included with sponsorship ($10,000-$50,000+) or available for purchase ($5,000-$20,000). **Upside**: Official data, high accuracy. **Downside**: Expensive, slow delivery, limited to sponsors.

### 2. [Search LinkedIn Manually](/compare/whogoes-vs-manual-linkedin-search)

Search for posts mentioning "HIMSS," "HIMSS26," or "#HIMSS2026." Scroll. Copy. Paste into spreadsheet. Repeat.

**Cost**: Free (plus 4-8 hours of your time). **Upside**: Real proof of attendance, no cost. **Downside**: Painfully slow, no structured data export, and LinkedIn's algorithm means you'll miss the majority of attendees who posted.

### 3. Use the Official HIMSS Event App

The HIMSS conference app and the [MapYourShow](https://himss26.mapyourshow.com) exhibitor directory let you browse exhibitors and some attendee profiles. Attendee profiles are opt-in and limited.

**Cost**: Free with registration. **Upside**: Official source. **Downside**: Incomplete coverage, no bulk export, no email data.

### 4. Buy from Third-Party Data Vendors

Several companies sell compiled HIMSS attendee and exhibitor databases built from registration data, badge scans, and public directories.

**Cost**: $250-$5,000+. **Upside**: Large contact volumes. **Downside**: Accuracy is all over the place. 30-50% email bounce rates are common with these vendors. No proof of attendance, and the data might be months old.

### 5. Use a LinkedIn-Proof Attendee Platform

Platforms like WhoGoes compile attendee lists by tracking public LinkedIn posts where people mention attending specific events. Every contact links back to their post as proof.

**Cost**: From $29 for 200 contacts (5 free previews). **Upside**: LinkedIn proof, verified emails, fast access. **Downside**: Limited to people who posted publicly.
