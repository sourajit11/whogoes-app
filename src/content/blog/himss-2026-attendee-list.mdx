## How Do I Get the HIMSS 2026 Attendee List?

There are five main ways to get HIMSS attendee data. Each varies in cost, accuracy, and time investment.

### 1. Request It from the Event Organizer

HIMSS shares attendee data with sponsors and exhibitors under specific agreements. If your company exhibited or sponsored the event, you may have access to lead retrieval data from badge scans.

- **Cost**: Included with sponsorship ($10,000-$50,000+) or available for purchase ($5,000-$20,000)
- **Pros**: Official data, high accuracy
- **Cons**: Expensive, limited to sponsors/exhibitors, often delayed delivery

### 2. Search LinkedIn Manually

You can search LinkedIn for posts mentioning "HIMSS," "HIMSS26," or "#HIMSS2026" and manually build a list of attendees who publicly shared that they attended.

- **Cost**: Free (plus your time)
- **Pros**: No financial investment, real proof of attendance
- **Cons**: Extremely time-consuming (4-8 hours per event), limited to people who posted, no structured data export

### 3. Use the Official HIMSS Event App

The HIMSS conference app and the MapYourShow exhibitor directory let you browse exhibitors and some attendee profiles. However, attendee profiles are typically opt-in and limited.

- **Cost**: Free with registration
- **Pros**: Official source, some networking features
- **Cons**: Incomplete attendee coverage, no bulk export, no email/phone data

### 4. Buy from Third-Party Data Vendors

Several companies sell compiled HIMSS attendee and exhibitor databases. These lists are typically built from registration data, badge scans, and public directories.

- **Cost**: $250-$5,000+ depending on vendor and list size
- **Pros**: Large contact volumes, includes emails and phone numbers
- **Cons**: Accuracy varies widely (30-50% email bounce rates common), no proof of attendance, may include outdated data
