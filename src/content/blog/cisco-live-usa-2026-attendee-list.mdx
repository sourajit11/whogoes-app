## What IT Buyers Are Evaluating at Cisco Live 2026

Cisco Live isn't just a product launch event. It's where enterprise IT teams go to evaluate their entire infrastructure roadmap. Budgets follow themes. The themes driving attendance in 2026 map directly to active purchasing cycles.

**AI-powered networking.** Cisco has gone all-in on AI across its portfolio. AI Network Analytics, predictive troubleshooting, and AIOps for campus and data center networks are dominating the session tracks. According to [Cisco's 2026 Global Networking Trends Report](https://www.cisco.com/c/en/us/solutions/enterprise-networks/networking-technology-trends.html), 65% of enterprises plan to adopt AI-assisted network management within 18 months. Buyers at these sessions are actively evaluating, not just browsing.

**SASE and zero-trust security.** Cisco's Secure Access Service Edge (SASE) platform, along with zero-trust network access, XDR, and cloud-delivered security, draws a heavy CISO and security architect audience. Post-pandemic remote work made SASE a board-level priority. The attendees in these tracks are typically 6-12 months from a purchasing decision.

**Hybrid cloud and multi-cloud.** Cisco's partnerships with AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud make Cisco Live a key venue for hybrid cloud strategy discussions. Attendees include cloud architects, infrastructure VPs, and platform engineering leads evaluating networking across multiple cloud providers.

**Network automation and observability.** The shift from manual CLI-based network management to automation platforms (Cisco DNA Center, Meraki, ThousandEyes) is driving a distinct buyer segment: network operations managers and automation engineers looking to reduce MTTR and scale without headcount.

**Sustainability in IT infrastructure.** Energy-efficient networking, power management in data centers, and Scope 2/3 emissions from IT operations are increasingly on the agenda. This is newer territory but growing fast, driven by ESG reporting mandates.

Context matters here. Knowing which tracks your prospect cares about shapes your outreach. A CISO evaluating SASE is a different conversation than a network engineer looking at automation tooling.

## Inside the Cisco Live Attendee Mix: Roles, Segments, and Buying Authority

Cisco Live attracts a diverse but concentrated audience. Here's how the attendee base breaks down.

### Network Engineering and Architecture

The largest segment. Senior network engineers, network architects, and infrastructure leads who design and manage enterprise networks. These are the technical evaluators. They don't sign purchase orders, but nothing gets bought without their approval.

**Typical titles:** Sr. Network Engineer, Network Architect, Infrastructure Lead, Principal Engineer, Solutions Architect

**What they're evaluating:** SD-WAN platforms, campus switching, wireless (Wi-Fi 7), network automation, ACI for data centers

### IT Leadership and Management

Directors and VPs who own the infrastructure budget. They attend keynotes and strategy sessions, walk the World of Solutions hall, and take meetings with vendors.

**Typical titles:** IT Director, VP of Infrastructure, CTO, VP of Technology, Head of IT Operations

**What they care about:** TCO, vendor consolidation, multi-year licensing, platform vs. point-solution decisions

### Security Leaders

CISOs, security directors, and architects who manage the security stack. Cisco's acquisition spree in security (Splunk, Duo, Thousand Eyes integration) has expanded this audience segment significantly.

**Typical titles:** CISO, Director of Security Operations, Security Architect, VP of Information Security

**What they're evaluating:** SASE, XDR, zero-trust architecture, SOC automation, threat intelligence platforms

### Cloud and DevOps

Cloud architects and platform engineers evaluating hybrid and multi-cloud networking. This segment has grown steadily as enterprises move workloads across AWS, Azure, and GCP while keeping on-prem infrastructure.

**Typical titles:** Cloud Architect, Platform Engineer, DevOps Lead, Director of Cloud Infrastructure

**What they care about:** Cloud networking, Kubernetes networking, hybrid connectivity, infrastructure-as-code

### Service Providers and Partners

A significant chunk of Cisco Live attendees are from Cisco partners, managed service providers, and telcos. They're there for partner programs, certifications, and pre-sales training.

**Typical titles:** Solutions Engineer, Pre-Sales Architect, MSP Director, Partner Account Manager

**Why this matters for you:** If you sell through the channel or complement Cisco's portfolio, these are your ecosystem contacts.

## Cisco Live Buying Signals to Watch For

Not every attendee is in buying mode. Some are browsing. Others are buying. These signals help you prioritize who to reach out to.

**Session attendance patterns.** Someone who attends three SASE sessions and a zero-trust lab is further along in their security evaluation than someone who only hit the keynote. If you can reference the specific topic track in your outreach, you're speaking their language.

**LinkedIn posts about specific sessions.** A post like "Great deep dive on Cisco SD-WAN at Cisco Live" tells you exactly what they're evaluating. That's a warm lead with a built-in conversation starter.

**Job changes in the last 6 months.** A new CISO or IT Director attending Cisco Live is likely evaluating their inherited tech stack. New leaders buy. Classic pattern.

**Company signals.** Check if the prospect's company recently announced a cloud migration, data center build, or security incident. These external triggers combined with Cisco Live attendance suggest active evaluation.

**Cisco certification activity.** Attendees pursuing CCNP, CCIE, or DevNet certifications are deeply embedded in the Cisco ecosystem. They influence vendor decisions even if they don't hold the budget.
