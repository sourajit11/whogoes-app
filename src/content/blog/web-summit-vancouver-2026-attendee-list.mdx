## Web Summit Vancouver 2026: Who's in the Room

**Web Summit Vancouver 2026 expected attendance:** approximately 15,000 attendees across three days, based on organizer projections for the inaugural Vancouver edition. The number of attendees is smaller than Lisbon's peak of roughly 70,000 because Vancouver is a new city with a more focused positioning. Vancouver is new. Organizers keep it tight. The expected attendees skew founder-heavy, with 2,000+ startups participating through the ALPHA and BETA programs alone, plus 1,000+ investors, 400+ media members, and a growing contingent of enterprise buyers from Fortune 500 technology teams who travel for innovation scouting and partnership discussions.

Scale defines Web Summit. Every segment of tech is represented, but the concentration varies by attendee type. Here's the breakdown.

### Startup Founders and CTOs

This is Web Summit's center of gravity. The ALPHA program (early-stage) and BETA program (growth-stage) bring 2,000+ startups to the expo floor. Founders pitch on stage. CTOs demo products. The energy is intense.

**Typical titles:** CEO/Founder, CTO, Co-Founder, VP of Product

**What they're doing there:** Fundraising, partnerships, press coverage, customer acquisition. Many are in active fundraising rounds and open to conversations about tooling, infrastructure, and go-to-market support.

### Investors and VCs

Web Summit actively recruits investors. Over 1,000 are expected in 2026, from seed-stage angels to growth equity firms. Money follows founders. Deals happen fast. They take meetings through the Web Summit app, attend investor-only sessions, and scout the ALPHA/BETA expo.

**Typical titles:** Partner, Managing Director, Principal, Investment Director

**Why this matters for sales teams:** If you sell to VC-backed startups, the investors at Web Summit are your channel. They recommend tools to their portfolio companies.

### Enterprise Technology Executives

The less visible but high-value segment. Big budgets, low profiles. Don't overlook them. CTOs, CIOs, and VPs of Engineering from Fortune 500 companies attend Web Summit for innovation scouting. They're quieter than the startup crowd but they control procurement.

**Typical titles:** CTO, CIO, VP of Engineering, Chief Digital Officer, Head of Innovation

**What they're evaluating:** AI platforms, cloud infrastructure, cybersecurity, developer tools, and emerging technology for internal R&D

### Media and Content Creators

Press loves Web Summit. Tech journalists, analysts, podcasters, and LinkedIn influencers attend and post actively. Coverage is massive. Their posts create secondary awareness for products and companies mentioned throughout the event.

## Dublin Tech Summit 2026: Who's in the Room

Different vibe entirely. Dublin Tech Summit is smaller, more European, more enterprise-focused. Quality over quantity.

### European Enterprise Buyers

Dublin punches hard. Enterprise runs deep. The city hosts European HQs of Google, Meta, Microsoft, Salesforce, and hundreds of SaaS companies, so the attendee mix skews toward enterprise technology leaders based in EMEA.

**Typical titles:** VP of Engineering, Director of IT, Head of Data, CTO (mid-market)

**What they're evaluating:** SaaS platforms, data infrastructure, AI/ML tooling, compliance solutions (GDPR-heavy audience)

### Irish and UK Tech Leaders

Talent stays local. Roots run deep. Ireland has become a European tech hub, and Dublin Tech Summit is the anchor event for Irish and UK technology leaders who may not travel to Vancouver or Lisbon.

**Typical titles:** CTO, Head of Product, Engineering Director, Chief Data Officer

**Why this matters:** If your target market includes UK and Ireland, Dublin Tech Summit has a higher density of these buyers than Web Summit.

### Mid-Market Decision-Makers

The sweet spot. Where Web Summit attracts startup founders and enterprise C-suite, Dublin Tech Summit fills the gap with mid-market companies (100-2,000 employees). These attendees are often the actual decision-makers, not just influencers, and they move faster on purchasing decisions. Shorter sales cycles.

### Investors (European Focus)

Less noise here. Dublin Tech Summit attracts European VCs, family offices, and angel investors. The investor density is lower than Web Summit's, but the conversations are more focused. Less competition for attention.

## Which Conference Delivers Better Leads?

Depends on your buyer. Here's the decision framework:

### Choose Web Summit Vancouver If:

Think massive. Scale wins here.

- **Your target market is global.** 160+ countries represented. If you sell worldwide, Web Summit gives you the broadest reach in one event.
- **You sell to startups and VC-backed companies.** 2,000+ startups + 1,000+ investors = the densest startup ecosystem gathering of the year.
- **Brand visibility matters.** Web Summit generates massive LinkedIn activity and press coverage. Your presence (even without a booth) gets amplified.
- **You need volume.** 40,000+ attendees means a larger addressable list, even after ICP filtering.

### Choose Dublin Tech Summit If:

Think focused, not massive. Less is more.

- **Your target market is Europe.** The attendee base is concentrated in EMEA. If you're breaking into the European market, Dublin is more efficient.
- **You sell to mid-market enterprise.** Dublin Tech Summit has a higher proportion of mid-market decision-makers who buy faster and with fewer stakeholders.
- **You want quality conversations.** A 10,000-person event is easier to navigate than a 40,000-person one. More serendipitous meetings. Shorter lines at sessions.
- **Budget matters.** Lower ticket price, cheaper travel (especially from Europe), and a shorter event (2 days vs. 4).

### Work Both If:

You sell to tech companies across size and geography, and your team can support two events in three weeks. The audiences overlap slightly but are distinct enough that both deliver unique contacts. Cross-reference the attendee lists to find people going to both shows. Those are your most engaged, most connected prospects.
