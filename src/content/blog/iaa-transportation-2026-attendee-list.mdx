## The Exhibitor Landscape: What 1,700 Companies Are Bringing to Hannover

IAA Transportation isn't a niche event. It's big. Really big. The show is the global gravity center for commercial vehicles, and the exhibitor mix reflects that, spanning the truck and van OEMs that anchor the main halls, the tier-one suppliers filling the component pavilions, and the electric and hydrogen drivetrain firms that now occupy entire hall sections of their own. I've broken it down into the categories that matter most if you're selling into transportation and logistics.

### OEM Heavyweights

The truck and van OEMs anchor the show. [Daimler Truck](https://www.daimlertruck.com), [MAN Truck & Bus](https://www.man.eu), [Scania](https://www.scania.com), [Volvo Trucks](https://www.volvotrucks.com), [Iveco](https://www.iveco.com), and [DAF Trucks](https://www.daf.com) all take massive hall space. These booths aren't just product displays. They're where fleet procurement teams go to evaluate next-generation drivetrains, compare total cost of ownership, and start conversations that turn into multi-year contracts.

Worth watching in 2026: [BYD](https://www.byd.com) and [Hyundai](https://www.hyundai.com) are expanding their European commercial vehicle presence. Chinese manufacturers are also showing up in larger numbers, which is reshaping the competitive landscape for European fleet operators.

### Tier 1 Suppliers and Component Makers

ZF, Bosch, Continental, Knorr-Bremse, and WABCO (now part of ZF) fill the supplier halls. If you're selling software, sensors, or services to these companies, IAA is where their engineering and procurement teams are most accessible. They're evaluating partners, not just showcasing products.

### EV and Hydrogen Drivetrain Companies

This category has exploded since 2022. Battery pack manufacturers, hydrogen fuel cell developers, and e-axle suppliers now occupy entire hall sections. According to [IAA organizers](https://www.iaa-transportation.com/en), charging infrastructure and hydrogen are two of the three central themes for 2026. That's not marketing fluff; it reflects where the exhibitor registrations are coming from.

### Logistics Tech and Fleet Software Vendors

Telematics providers, fleet management SaaS companies, route optimization platforms, ELD/tachograph solution vendors, and warehouse-to-last-mile software players. This is where [SDRs focused on pre-event prospecting](/blog/trade-show-attendee-data-sdrs) should concentrate. The buyers walking these aisles are fleet managers and logistics directors actively evaluating tools.

<Callout type="tip">
The logistics tech halls tend to be less crowded than the OEM pavilions, but the buyer density is higher. Fleet managers spend more time here because they're comparing multiple vendors in a single visit.
</Callout>

## Conference Tracks: Where IAA Transportation Buyers Reveal What They're Evaluating

The [IAA Conference](https://www.iaa-transportation.com/en/exhibitors/iaa-formats/iaa-conference) runs across two main stages plus an Industry Stage reserved for exhibitors. Most sessions are in English and open to all visitors. English, not German. That matters. That's unusual for a German trade show, and it tells you something about the audience: this is a genuinely international event.

The 2026 programme centers on these tracks:

### Electrification and Charging Infrastructure

Battery-electric trucks have moved from concept to production. The conversation has shifted from "should we electrify?" to "how do we manage depot charging for a 200-truck fleet?" If you sell charging hardware, energy management software, or grid integration services, the people in these sessions are your buyers. Period.

### Hydrogen and Alternative Fuels

Hydrogen fuel cells for long-haul remain a live debate. OEMs are split on timelines, and fleet operators are split on economics. The sessions covering hydrogen refueling infrastructure and total cost comparisons with battery-electric attract the most skeptical, most informed attendees. These are the people who've done the math.

### Autonomous Transport and AI

Self-driving trucks, platooning, and AI-driven logistics optimization. I've noticed that the attendees in these sessions skew toward innovation and strategy roles rather than procurement. Good for relationship building, less immediate for pipeline.

### Urban Logistics and Last-Mile Delivery

E-cargo bikes, micro-hubs, consolidation centers. This track draws a different crowd than the long-haul sessions. Think urban planners, last-mile delivery operators, and municipal transport officials alongside the logistics companies.

<Callout type="tip">
Session attendance data is gold for SDRs. If someone attends a session on "Fleet Electrification ROI for Mid-Sized Operators," you know exactly what they care about. Use session themes to personalize your outreach after the show.
</Callout>

## Working the Hannover Floor: A Practical Strategy for Sales Teams

The Hannover Fairgrounds are massive. Twenty-plus halls, indoor and outdoor areas, test tracks. You can't cover it all, and trying to will waste your week. A single day spent wandering the wrong halls is a day you don't get back, which is why the teams that leave Hannover with real pipeline are the ones that decided which two or three halls matched their ICP before they ever booked the flight.

### Pick Your Halls Before You Arrive

Don't wing it. The IAA floor plan clusters exhibitors by category. OEMs are in the main halls. Suppliers and component makers occupy adjacent halls. The logistics tech and startup areas are typically in separate buildings. Decide which two or three halls matter for your ICP and commit.

I've seen teams lose entire days walking the wrong halls because they didn't check the floor plan. It's free on the [IAA website](https://www.iaa-transportation.com/en/visitors). Use it.

### Mornings Beat Afternoons

European trade shows follow a rhythm. The first two hours after opening are the quietest. By mid-afternoon, the halls are packed and nobody wants another conversation. If you're trying to get quality face time with a fleet procurement director, be at their booth at 9:30, not 3:00.

### The Press Day Hack

September 14 is press day. It's technically for media, but many exhibitors have their senior leadership on-site. If you have exhibitor credentials or media access, this is the least crowded, highest-quality networking window of the entire event.

### Use the Outdoor Test Tracks

Over 6,300 test drives happened at IAA 2024, according to [IAA organizers](https://www.iaa-transportation.com/en/press/press-releases/iaa-press-releases/iaa-transportation-2024-sets-new-records-clear-message). The test track areas attract fleet operators who want to evaluate vehicles firsthand. These people aren't browsing. They're buying. Conversations at the test track tend to be more genuine because the booth formality drops away.

## Spotting Buying Intent on the IAA Floor

Not every attendee is a buyer. Some are there for the conference. Some are students. Some are competitors doing recon. If you're spending credits on an attendee list or investing a week in Hannover, you need to filter for real purchase intent. Filter hard. The whole return on a week in Hannover or a stack of unlocked contacts comes down to whether you can separate the fleet director who is mid-evaluation from the student, the journalist, and the competitor walking the same aisle.

### The Exhibitor Type Filter

Certain exhibitor categories attract certain buyer types. A fleet management SaaS booth pulls in fleet managers. A charging infrastructure booth pulls in facility managers and sustainability directors. If someone visits three booths in the same category, that's a pattern, not a coincidence.

### LinkedIn Posts Tell You More Than Badge Scans

This is where [verified attendee data](/blog/how-to-verify-trade-show-attendance) gets interesting. When someone posts on LinkedIn that they're heading to IAA Transportation to "evaluate electric truck options for our Central European fleet," that's a public buying signal. It tells you their role, their company, their focus area, and their timing. Badge scan data from the organizer can't give you that.

### Conference Session Choices as Intent Signals

Attendees who register for sessions on "Charging Infrastructure ROI" or "Hydrogen vs. Battery-Electric for Long-Haul" are further along in their evaluation than someone attending a general keynote. If you can identify which sessions a prospect attended (and LinkedIn posts often mention specific sessions), you can tailor your follow-up with precision.

The strongest buying signal at IAA isn't booth visits or badge scans. It's a LinkedIn post that names a specific technology or challenge. "Excited to see the latest depot charging solutions at IAA Transportation" is a prospect telling you exactly what to talk about.

## IAA Transportation vs. Other Mobility Trade Shows

If you're deciding where to focus your prospecting budget, it helps to know how IAA Transportation stacks up against similar events.

| Feature | IAA Transportation (Hannover) | IAA Mobility (Munich) | Automechanika (Frankfurt) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Focus | Commercial vehicles, logistics, transport | Passenger mobility, future transport | Aftermarket, repair, maintenance |
| Attendees | ~145,000 | ~500,000 (public + trade) | ~78,000 |
| Exhibitors | ~1,700 | ~750 | ~2,800 |
| Buyer profile | Fleet operators, logistics directors, procurement | OEM engineers, mobility startups, consumers | Workshop owners, parts distributors |
| Frequency | Biennial (even years) | Biennial (odd years) | Biennial (even years) |
| Best for B2B sales | Fleet software, EV infrastructure, logistics tech | Connected car, MaaS platforms | Parts, tools, diagnostics |

IAA Transportation is the clear winner if your ICP includes fleet operators, logistics companies, or commercial vehicle OEMs. Size is a trap. A show with half a million attendees is worthless to you if the overwhelming majority are consumers and enthusiasts rather than the fleet operators, procurement leads, and logistics directors who actually sign the purchase orders. IAA Mobility (Munich) draws a bigger crowd, but a huge chunk of that is public visitors, not buyers. Automechanika targets the aftermarket, which is a completely different sale.

Side note: IAA Transportation and IAA Mobility are both organized by the [VDA (German Association of the Automotive Industry)](https://www.vda.de/en), but they're separate events with separate audiences. I've talked to SDRs who confused the two and wasted outreach on the wrong contacts. Don't be that person.
