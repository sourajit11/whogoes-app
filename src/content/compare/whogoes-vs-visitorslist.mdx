## What VisitorsList Does Well

VisitorsList has been in the attendee data space for a while and has some strengths:

- **Custom filtering**: Filter by industry, job title, company size, geography, and other criteria within an event's attendee list
- **Broad event coverage**: Covers major trade shows across industries
- **Multiple data points**: Includes company information, contact details, and demographics
- **CRM-ready formats**: Data delivered in formats compatible with major CRMs
- **Dedicated account management**: Quote-based model means you get a human to work with

For enterprise teams that want a managed, customized data delivery with specific filtering, VisitorsList's approach has appeal.

## Where WhoGoes Wins

### 1. Transparent pricing

WhoGoes costs $29 for 200 contacts. That price is on the website. No sales call, no quote request, no back-and-forth negotiation. You know what you're paying before you sign up.

VisitorsList uses quote-based pricing, which typically means higher minimums and a longer procurement process. If you're an SDR who needs a list for next week's trade show, waiting for a quote isn't ideal.

### 2. LinkedIn proof you can verify

This is the big differentiator. WhoGoes links every contact to their public LinkedIn post about the event. You can click through, see the post, and confirm the person actually attended or plans to attend.

With VisitorsList's proprietary verification, you're trusting the vendor's word. The data might be accurate, but you can't independently verify it. When you reference an event in your outreach ("I saw you were at HIMSS"), you want to be sure that's true.

### 3. Instant delivery with free preview

WhoGoes shows you 5 contacts free per event before you pay anything. You can evaluate the data quality, see the LinkedIn proof, and decide if the list is worth unlocking. Instant access, no waiting.

VisitorsList delivers within 24-48 hours after you request a quote and place an order. That's fine for planned campaigns, but not great when you need a list quickly.

### 4. Low barrier to entry

$29, no contract, no minimum commitment. An individual SDR can expense it. A freelance consultant can afford it. A startup founder doing their own outreach can try it without budget approval.

Quote-based pricing with no public price list signals enterprise sales cycles: procurement, negotiation, possibly a contract. Different buying experience.

## The Proof Question

This deserves its own section because it's the fundamental philosophical difference.

**VisitorsList's approach**: "We've verified this data through our proprietary process. Trust us, these people attended."

**WhoGoes' approach**: "Here's the LinkedIn post where this person said they're attending. Click through and see for yourself."

Both can deliver accurate data. But when you're doing cold outreach and referencing an event, LinkedIn proof eliminates the risk of getting it wrong. "I saw your post about attending NRF" is a conversation starter that's verifiably true. If the proof doesn't exist, the contact isn't in the list.

This matters because nothing kills credibility faster than referencing an event someone didn't attend.
