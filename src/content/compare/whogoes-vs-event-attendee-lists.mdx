## The Problem With Organizer Lists

Event organizer lists have three major drawbacks:

1. **Cost** — $5K–$20K per event, usually bundled with sponsorship. For companies attending 5–10 events per year, that's $25K–$200K just for attendee data.
2. **Timing** — Most organizers share lists days or weeks after the event. By then, your competitors have already followed up.
3. **Quality** — Organizer lists include everyone who registered, including no-shows and people who used fake emails at registration. There's no way to verify who actually attended.

## Why LinkedIn Proof Matters

WhoGoes takes a different approach. Instead of relying on registration data, it monitors public LinkedIn posts about trade shows. Every contact in a WhoGoes list has verifiable proof that they engaged with the event — they posted about it, shared photos, or mentioned attending.

This means you're reaching out to people who were genuinely engaged, not just names on a registration form.

## Who Should Buy Organizer Lists?

Organizer lists still make sense if:

- You're a **title sponsor** and the list is included in your package
- You need **100% of registrants** regardless of engagement level
- The event is very small (under 100 people) and WhoGoes may not have coverage
