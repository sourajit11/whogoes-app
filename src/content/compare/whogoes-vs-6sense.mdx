> **Quick answer:** 6sense is an enterprise ABM platform that identifies anonymous buying signals at the account level ($50K to $100K+/yr). WhoGoes provides named individual contacts from specific trade shows with verified emails and LinkedIn proof ($29). For event-based prospecting, WhoGoes is more actionable and roughly 1/1000th the cost.

6sense and WhoGoes both help B2B sales teams find engaged prospects, but at wildly different price points and for different reasons. I'll break down where each one makes sense.

**6sense** is an enterprise ABM platform. It uses AI to de-anonymize website visitors, track buying signals across channels, and predict which accounts are ready to buy. Comprehensive, powerful, and expensive: typically $50K to $100K+/yr.

**WhoGoes** is an event attendee data platform. It gives you individual contacts who attended specific trade shows, with verified emails and LinkedIn proof. Focused, fast, and affordable: starting at $29.

## Key Takeaways

- 6sense identifies account-level buying signals across all channels for $50K+/yr; WhoGoes gives you individual contacts with emails from specific events for $29
- 6sense requires a separate contact database to find the right people; WhoGoes includes names, emails, and LinkedIn profiles
- 6sense needs weeks of setup and CRM integration; WhoGoes delivers value in minutes
- For event-based prospecting specifically, WhoGoes is more actionable at a fraction of the cost

| Feature | WhoGoes | 6sense |
|---|---|---|
| **Data type** | Individual contacts with emails | Account-level intent + predictions |
| **Price** | From $29 (pay-as-you-go) | $50,000 to $100,000+/yr |
| **Contact info** | Name, email, company, LinkedIn | Account names (contacts extra) |

| **Signal types** | Event attendance only | Web visits, content, ads, all channels |

## What 6sense Does Well

Credit where it's due. 6sense is a powerful enterprise platform:

- **Multi-channel intent**: Tracks buying signals across web visits, ad engagement, content consumption, and third-party data
- **Predictive AI**: Scores accounts by buying stage (awareness through decision)
- **Website de-anonymization**: Identifies companies visiting your website, even without form fills
- **CRM/MAP integration**: Deep integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, Marketo, and more
- **Orchestration**: Automates workflows based on buying signals

For enterprise ABM teams with the budget, 6sense does a lot. No argument there.

## Where WhoGoes Wins

That said, 6sense has real blind spots that WhoGoes fills.

### 1. Individual-level data

6sense tells you "Acme Corp is showing buying signals." Okay, great. But who at Acme Corp? Which person? What's their email?

WhoGoes tells you "Sarah Chen, VP of Sales at Acme Corp, attended [HIMSS 2026](/events/himss-2026). Here's her email and LinkedIn proof." That's the difference between a signal and something you can actually act on.

### 2. Immediate actionability

With 6sense, you get account signals and then need to find the right contacts, research their interests, and craft outreach. That workflow can take days. With WhoGoes, you download a list of verified contacts with emails and start sending within the hour.

### 3. Price accessibility

At $50K+/yr, 6sense is out of reach for most startups and SMBs. I've talked to teams that wanted 6sense but couldn't justify the spend for their first year. WhoGoes starts at $29 with no contracts, which means event-based prospecting is accessible to any team size.

### 4. Speed to value

6sense requires weeks of setup, CRM integration, and training before you see results. WhoGoes delivers value in minutes: search for an event, preview contacts free, unlock the list. Done.

## Who Should Use 6sense?

6sense makes sense for enterprise companies that:

- Have **$50K+/yr** budget for intent data tooling
- Run **multi-channel ABM programs** that need centralized orchestration
- Need **predictive scoring** to prioritize thousands of accounts
- Want to **de-anonymize website traffic** and track the full buyer journey

If that's you, 6sense earns its price tag. But most teams I talk to don't need all of that.

## Who Should Use WhoGoes?

WhoGoes is built for teams that:

- Need **individual contacts** from specific trade shows and conferences
- Want **verified emails** and **LinkedIn proof** of attendance
- Prefer **pay-as-you-go** over annual enterprise contracts
- Value **speed**: attendee lists in minutes, not months
