> **Quick answer:** WhoGoes and JoinLTO serve different needs. WhoGoes is a self-service platform: browse 1,200+ events, preview 5 contacts free, unlock attendee data at $29 for 200 contacts. JoinLTO is a full-service prospecting solution: they build ICP-matched lists, run pre-event outreach campaigns, deploy targeted ads, and provide on-site lead capture tools. WhoGoes is for teams that want data fast. JoinLTO is for teams that want a managed prospecting service around trade shows.

These two products solve related but different problems. Comparing them directly on price or features misses the point — they're built for different buyers with different needs.

**[JoinLTO](https://www.joinlto.com/)** is a full-service trade show prospecting platform. They build custom attendee and exhibitor lists matched to your ICP, run pre-event outreach campaigns, deploy surround-sound digital advertising to your prospect list, and provide ShowScout, their on-site lead capture app. It's an end-to-end managed service for maximizing trade show ROI.

**WhoGoes** is a self-service attendee data platform. It sources contacts from public LinkedIn posts where people mention attending events. Every contact comes with proof of attendance. Transparent pricing at $29 for 200 contacts, instant access.

- WhoGoes includes LinkedIn proof of attendance; JoinLTO provides double-verified contact data
- JoinLTO adds outreach, advertising, and on-site tools; WhoGoes provides data only

## What JoinLTO Does Well

JoinLTO is built for teams that want a full-service trade show prospecting partner, not just a data provider:

- **ICP-matched lists**: Every list is built around your specific targeting criteria — industry, company size, revenue, technology stack, job titles, and geography. You're not getting a generic attendee dump. You're getting a curated list of qualified prospects.
- **Pre-event outreach**: JoinLTO doesn't just hand you a list. They run multi-channel B2B demand generation to warm up prospects before the show. Your team shows up with conversations already started.
- **Surround marketing**: Targeted digital ads served to your prospect list before the event. Your company name is familiar before your team walks the floor.
- **ShowScout lead capture**: Their on-site app helps sales reps capture and qualify leads during the event. Integrates with the pre-event list so reps know which prospects they've already been warming up.
- **Exhibitor lists**: JoinLTO provides both attendee and exhibitor lists, useful for companies selling to exhibitors or evaluating partnership opportunities.
- **End-to-end ROI tracking**: Because JoinLTO manages the full funnel (list → outreach → ads → on-site → follow-up), they can track the entire pipeline from first touch to closed deal.

For enterprise teams with significant trade show budgets and dedicated event marketing staff, JoinLTO's managed approach removes execution burden. You pay a premium, but you get a turnkey system.

## Where WhoGoes Wins

### 1. Transparent pricing that works for any budget

WhoGoes costs $29 for 200 contacts. No quote request, no sales call, no scoping conversation. You know the price before you sign up.

JoinLTO is a premium managed service with quote-based pricing. The full-service bundle (lists + outreach + ads + lead capture) is designed for teams investing thousands per event. For an SDR who needs an attendee list for next week's trade show, that procurement process doesn't fit the timeline.

### 2. LinkedIn proof of attendance

WhoGoes links every contact to their public LinkedIn post about the event. Click through, see the post, confirm attendance. When your outreach says "I noticed you're heading to [HIMSS](/events/himss-2026)," you know it's true because you can see their LinkedIn post.

JoinLTO provides double-verified contact data matched to your ICP. The data is accurate, but you can't independently verify that a specific person attended a specific event. That verification matters when your outreach strategy depends on event-specific personalization.

### 3. Instant, self-service access

Browse events, preview 5 contacts free, unlock the full list — all in minutes. No onboarding call, no account manager, no scoping document.

JoinLTO's managed model requires engagement setup, ICP definition, and list building time. That's the right approach for a $50K trade show investment. It's the wrong approach when you need a list today.

### 4. Coverage across 1,200+ events

WhoGoes tracks over 1,200 trade shows and conferences. Browse the full catalog at [/events](/events). New events are added continuously based on LinkedIn activity.

JoinLTO builds lists on demand for specific events, which means each new event requires a new engagement. For teams covering many events across different verticals, self-service access is faster.

## When JoinLTO Is the Better Choice

JoinLTO wins in three scenarios:

**You need exhibitor data, not just attendee data.** WhoGoes focuses on attendee data from LinkedIn posts. JoinLTO provides both attendee and exhibitor lists. If you sell to companies exhibiting at trade shows (booth equipment, logistics, lead capture tools), JoinLTO covers that use case.

**You want managed outreach, not just data.** If your team doesn't have the bandwidth to run pre-event email sequences, JoinLTO's managed outreach handles that. You get warm prospects instead of cold contacts.

**Your trade show budget justifies premium services.** When a single trade show costs $30K-$100K in booth, travel, and sponsorship fees, spending several thousand more on managed prospecting is a reasonable insurance policy. JoinLTO's end-to-end approach maximizes ROI on that investment.

## The Fundamental Difference

JoinLTO is a prospecting service that happens to include attendee data. WhoGoes is an attendee data platform that powers your own prospecting.

If you want someone to manage the entire trade show prospecting workflow — lists, outreach, ads, lead capture — JoinLTO does that. If you want verified attendee data with proof of attendance so you can run your own outreach, WhoGoes does that at a fraction of the cost.

Most growing sales teams start with self-service data and scale to managed services when trade show budgets justify it. WhoGoes serves both the starting point and the teams that prefer to keep outreach in-house.
